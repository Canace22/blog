重复

- **Original**: 重复
- **Translation**: Repeat
- **Explanation**: “重复” is a Chinese verb/noun meaning “to repeat” or “repetition”. It is translated directly into English as “Repeat” to preserve its concise and imperative tone, commonly used in contexts like instructions, commands (e.g., in software, music, or daily speech). No ambiguity here; it is a straightforward term without cultural specificity requiring additional context.
- **Example sentence**: 请不要重复同样的错误。 (English: Please do not repeat the same mistake.)

redundant

- **Original**: redundant
- **Translation**: 多余的
- **Explanation**: “Redundant” is an English adjective meaning unnecessary or superfluous, often implying something that exceeds what is required or repeats information needlessly. It is translated into Simplified Chinese as “多余的” to convey the sense of excess or being superfluous in a natural and commonly used way. In technical or formal contexts, it can also imply “冗余的” (e.g., in engineering or data), but “多余的” fits the general standalone word best here while preserving a neutral tone.
- **Example sentence**: This paragraph contains a lot of redundant information. (中文示例：这段文字包含很多多余的信息。)
